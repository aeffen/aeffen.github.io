Hi, I'm Audrey.

I'm an aspiring neuroscientist and incoming grad student at MIT.

I did my undergraduate studies at Harvard College, where I studied neurobiology and mathematical sciences. Of interest to some current students, I was in the very first cohort of LS 50, an experimental year-long integrated science course. During my undergrad years, I conducted research at Cellino Biotech, Biogen Inc, and Zhigang He's lab at Harvard Medical School and Boston Children's Hospital.

I've always been fascinated by how living things change, whether turning glucose into glyceraldehyde-3P or electrical impulses into dreams. Very, very broadly, my research interests lie in cell type-specific molecular and cellular mechanisms of neural degeneration, and how understanding those mechanisms can be leveraged for regeneration in the central nervous system. I'm also interested in decoding the complex interactions between neurons, glia, and the immune system.
