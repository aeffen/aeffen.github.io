Hi, I'm Audrey.

I'm an aspiring neuroscientist and grad student at MIT.

I did my undergrad at Harvard College, where I studied neurobiology and mathematical sciences. Of interest to some current students, I was in the very first cohort of LS50: Integrated Science, an experimental year-long integrated science course. I also TA for MCB80x: The Fundamentals of Neuroscience, an online course spanning cells to circuits and beyond.

I've always been fascinated by how living things change themselves and their environments, turning glucose into glyceraldehyde-3P and electrical impulses into dreams. During my undergrad years, I conducted research at Cellino Biotech, Biogen Inc, and Zhigang He's lab (HMS/BCH). I've worn many research hats, from stem cell biology and applied physics to pharmaceutics and molecular genetics. In my work, I hope to embody the nature of neuroscience itself: an inherently multidisciplinary field which uses different techniques and technologies to reveal the secrets of the nervous system invisible to a single approach alone.

Very, very broadly, my current research interests include the following:

  • Refining the CNS transcriptome
  • Neuro-glia-(immune) interactions
  • Glia in CNS health
  • Neurodegenerative disease
  • iPS modeling of the CNS
  • Stem cell engineering
